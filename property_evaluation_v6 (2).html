Scoring Field Definitions

All 13 criteria are normalized 0–10 against the 12 Wolf Ranch properties in this dataset. Weights (%) set the contribution of each field to the composite score. Raw MLS data: NTREIS.

💲 Affordability

List $/SF Default weight: 15%
MLS List Price ÷ Living Area (SqFt). Measures cost per square foot of living space. Lowest $/SF = score 10; highest = score 0. Rewards getting the most space for your dollar.
$189.82/sf → score 10 $279.60/sf → score 0

🏠 Property & Location

Living Area Default: 5%
Total finished interior SqFt from MLS. Larger homes score higher. Smallest (1,966 sf) = 0; largest (2,897 sf) = 10.
Year Built Default: 5%
Year of construction per MLS. Newer = higher score. Rewards lower maintenance risk, modern codes, updated systems. New Construction 2026 = score 10; oldest (2017) = score 0.
Lot Size Default: 4%
Total lot area in SqFt from MLS. Larger lots score higher — rewards yard space, privacy, and resale potential. 604 Pecan Bottom Trl = 11,979 sf (score 10); smallest = 6,059 sf (score 0).
Beds + Baths Default: 3%
Sum of bedroom + full bathroom count from MLS. More rooms = higher score. Sum of 7 (4 bed / 3 bath) = score 10; sum of 4 (2 bed / 2 bath) = score 0.
Construction Material Default: 4%
Exterior material from MLS "Construction" field. Scored on a fixed quality scale based on durability, maintenance cost, and resale value.
10 — Masonry-All Sides / Brick + Masonry 9 — Brick Veneer + Masonry + Stone Veneer 8 — Brick + Stucco / Stone + Stucco 7 — Brick only 6 — Siding-Wood + Stone
Flooring Quality Default: 8%
From MLS "Flooring" field. Rewards hard-surface flooring; penalizes carpet (maintenance, allergens, resale) and laminate (lower durability).
10 — No Carpet, Tile, Wood 7 — Carpet, Tile, Wood 4 — Carpet, Tile only 3 — Carpet, Laminate, Tile
Fireplace Default: 1%
Binary yes/no from MLS. Low weight reflects it is a comfort amenity rather than a structural or value driver. Fireplace present = 10; absent = 0.
View Quality Default: 15%
From MLS "View" field. Only meaningful views qualify. Generic views ("Neighborhood", "See Remarks") receive 0 — they describe standard suburban sightlines with no premium value.
10 — Park/Greenbelt, City View, Trees/Woods 0 — None, Neighborhood, See Remarks
Single-Story (Levels) Default: 22%
From MLS "Levels" field. One-story homes score 10 — easier accessibility, no stair noise between floors, simpler roof structure, and generally preferred in Georgetown TX's active-adult market. Two-story = 0. Highest-weighted criterion by default.
1-Story = 10 2-Story = 0

📐 Scoring Methodology

Each criterion is normalized to a 0–10 scale across the 10 properties. Continuous variables use linear normalization: score = (value − min) / (max − min) × 10, inverted for "lower is better" criteria. Binary criteria score 0 or 10. Tiered criteria use fixed lookup tables above.

Composite Score = Σ(criterion score × weight%) / 100. When all weights sum to 100%, the composite stays on the 0–10 scale. Use ⚙ Adjust Weights to reprioritize criteria to your preferences.
